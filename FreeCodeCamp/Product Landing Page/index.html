About Pinikpikan

Pinikpikan is a traditional dish of the Igorot people in the Cordillera region of the Philippines. It is known for its unique preparation method that involves lightly beating the chicken before cooking. This traditional dish is a cultural treasure that embodies the rich heritage of the Igorot people.

Preparation

The preparation of Pinikpikan involves a meticulous process that enhances the flavor and tenderness of the chicken. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  • Lightly beat the chicken with a stick to bruise the meat and bring out its natural flavors.
  • Slaughter the chicken and dress it carefully.
  • Roast the chicken over an open flame to singe the feathers and add a smoky flavor.
  • Cook the chicken with aromatics and spices until tender.

Ingredients

Pinikpikan is made with simple ingredients that come together to create a rich and flavorful dish:

  • 1 whole chicken
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Onions
  • Salt and pepper
  • Spring onions
  • Optional: Etag (cured pork) for added flavor
  • Optional: Chayote, for additional toppings
