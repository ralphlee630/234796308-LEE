Choosing the Components

Choosing the right components is critical to your PC's performance and longevity. Here are the key components you need:

  • Processor (CPU)
  • Motherboard
  • Memory (RAM)
  • Storage (SSD/HDD)
  • Graphics Card (GPU)
  • Power Supply (PSU)
  • Case
  • Cooling System

When selecting these components, consider compatibility and your specific needs, such as gaming, video editing, or general use.

Ensure that your chosen motherboard supports the CPU you plan to use. This is crucial for system stability.

let compatibilityCheck = (cpu, motherboard) => { return cpu.socket === motherboard.socket; }
Assembly

Assembling the PC involves several steps:

  1. Installing the CPU on the motherboard.
  2. Mounting the motherboard inside the case.
  3. Inserting the RAM into the motherboard slots.
  4. Installing the storage devices.
  5. Connecting the power supply to the motherboard and components.
  6. Installing the GPU.
  7. Connecting all necessary cables and managing cable organization.

Ensure you follow safety measures such as grounding yourself to prevent static damage to sensitive components.

Before beginning, gather all necessary tools like screwdrivers, and read the manuals that come with your components.

BIOS and OS Installation

After assembling the hardware, the next step is to configure the BIOS and install an operating system:

  • Power on the PC and enter the BIOS setup.
  • Configure boot sequence and other necessary settings.
  • Save changes and exit BIOS.
  • Insert the OS installation media (USB/DVD) and restart the PC.
  • Follow the on-screen instructions to install the OS.

Configuring the BIOS involves setting the correct boot priority for your storage devices and enabling any necessary features like XMP profiles for RAM.

Final Tips

Here are some final tips to ensure your PC building experience is smooth:

  • Double-check all connections before powering on the PC.
  • Keep your components' manuals handy for reference.
  • Take your time and work methodically to avoid mistakes.
  • Join PC building communities online for additional support and tips.

Remember to update all drivers and perform a stress test to ensure system stability after assembly.
